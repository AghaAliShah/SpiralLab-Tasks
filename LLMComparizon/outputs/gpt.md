#GPT 5.5
Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) is a technique that enhances large language models (LLMs) by retrieving relevant information from external data sources before generating responses. Introduced in 2020, it enables models to use current, domain-specific, or authoritative information beyond their training data, reducing retraining costs while improving transparency through source attribution. By retrieving documents from databases, uploaded files, or the web, RAG helps reduce hallucinations, although it cannot eliminate them entirely because models may still misinterpret retrieved content or combine conflicting information inaccurately. 

The RAG process converts reference data into vector embeddings stored in vector databases, retrieves the most relevant documents for a user query, and incorporates them into the prompt before generating a response. Performance can be improved through enhanced encoding, retriever optimization, reranking, hybrid search, chunking strategies, and redesigned language models. RAG is particularly valuable in applications requiring accurate, frequently updated information, such as healthcare, though challenges remain regarding evaluation and reliability. Despite improving factual grounding and reducing reliance on retraining, RAG systems may still hallucinate, fail to recognize uncertainty, or retrieve misleading information, making careful interpretation and source validation essential.
